## Zo Relationship Mapper

Hackathon #70 on the road to 100.

For Sup Build2026, I built Zo Relationship Mapper: a tool for turning cold job applications into warmer opportunity paths.

The flow is simple:
1. Paste your profile or resume
2. Rank live job opportunities
3. Click an opportunity
4. Find hiring managers, mentors, and people working in the role
5. Generate a contextual outreach draft

This came from a real career problem I keep seeing: opportunities are not only about fit. They are also about trust, timing, and knowing who to speak to.

Hackathon #70 is another rep in the journey from builder to operator. Less about just shipping features, more about testing whether an idea has a sharp user, painful workflow, and scalable wedge.

Built with React, TypeScript, Express, OpenAI, and Exa.

## Zo Expert

Published post:
https://www.instagram.com/p/DaFSaYHkwPC/?img_index=1

Hackathon #70 on the road to 100.

For Sup Build2026, I built Zo Expert: an AI owner proxy for SME owners who are still the main bottleneck in their business.

The idea is simple:
1. Capture the owner's services, FAQs, tone, and business rules
2. Let customers or staff ask routine questions
3. Answer in the owner's style
4. Escalate risky or sensitive questions back to the owner
5. Give the owner a clean brief on what needs attention

This came from a very operator-heavy problem: many small businesses do not lack knowledge, they lack scalable access to the owner's judgment.

The founder lesson here is that AI agents become more valuable when they are tied to a specific workflow, a clear owner, and a real escalation boundary. Not every answer should be automated. The product needs to know when to stop.

Built with React, TypeScript, Express, OpenAI, and Exa.
